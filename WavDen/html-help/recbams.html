recbams

 

Bayesian Denoising Procedure

 

DESCRIPTION

 
Denoising a signal using a Bayesian shrinkage method. It calls the bamsmooth procedure.

 

USAGE

 

f = recbams(signal, h)

 

REQUIRED ARGUMENTS

 

signal

 

1-d noisy signal, length(signal)= 2^J

h

 

Quadature mirror filter for wavelet transform

 

 

 

OPTIONAL ARGUMENTS

 

h    Optional, Default = Symmlet 8

 

VALUE

 

f           Estimate,  obtained by applying  a Bayesian wavelet shrinkage on the wavelet coefficients.

 

SIDE EFFECTS

 

BACKGROUND

 

This procedure addresses the problem of model-induced wavelet shrinkage.

Assuming the independence model according to which the wavelet coefficients

are treated  individually, a level-adaptive Bayesian model in the wavelet domain

is implemented . The model  has two important properties: (i) it realistically describes

empirical properties of signals in the wavelet domain, and (ii) it results in simple

optimal shrinkage rules to be used in fast  wavelet denoising. The proposed denoising

paradigm is called BAMS (short for Bayesian Adaptive Multiresolution Smoother).

 

REFERENCES

 

Vidakovic, B. & Ruggeri, F.  (2000). Bams Method : Theory and Simulations.

Discussion Paper, Institute of Statistics and Decision Sciences, Duke University,

USA.
